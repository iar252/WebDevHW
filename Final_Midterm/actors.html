Amitabh and Jaya Bachchan, basically the iconic mother and father of Bollywood. Catch them playing the parent role in any movie. What's more iconic than that? When they play both parents in a movie. I didn't get to watch movies of when they plays the main characters when they were younger, but I bet they were amazing. I've only ever seen then play the parent. I've seen Amitabh play so many different types of fathers, ranging from strict and controlling to a player who gets more women than his son. As far as I can recall, Jaya has always been the mother who children could always turn to for emotional support. The fact that they're both married in real life really just makes the whole thing a million times better.


As you can tell from my reviews, I'm pretty in love with SRK. He's done such beautiful work. When his character cries, there's no way the viewer isn't crying alongside him. His dialogue is amazing, and I get that the writers write it, but he really brings the words to life. Its probably his uniquely shaped eyebrows that do the trick. I'm glad he's been successfully entertaining us since such a young age. I feel almost priviliged to say that I've seen most of his classic movies. I cannot bring myself to actually accept that he's 51 years old now. HOW? Where did the time go??? Why can't he be young forever and ever? The funniest thing is that his wife's parents didn't want him to marry their daughter because he was a struggling artist back then. Little did they know she'd be marrying the King of Bollywood. :') Also, his precious life loving tweets just make your day a bit brighter. Like his personality is really great off camera too?? Bless.


Kajol is straight up iconic. People definitely knew her because of her eyebrows. (Who else had unique eyebrows? SRK. I'm started to see a trend amongst our talented actors..). But anyway, Kajol has those lovestruck eyes and playful personality that fits with all her characters. She'll win over the guy in the movie while winning over the audience as well. She's done so many movies with SRK that people have been shipping them since day one. If I didn't know about SRK's magical love story with his wife, I'd ship this one too. But anyway Kajol will always be remembered for her talent and I'm really sad she doesn't make as many movies as before. She was the queen of the 1990s and early 200s.


Saif Ali Khan almost always played the character that was playful or the younger brother that was mischievous. He played those roles the best because it fits his real life personality so well. He's probably my second favorite OG Khan of Bollywood. The movies he had with Rani Mukerjee worked out great, as the two worked well together. As a kid, I could always count on him to make sappy romantic movies funny.
